President Joe Biden and former President Donald Trump met at the White House on November 13, 2024, as part of the traditional presidential transition process. This meeting marked the first time Trump returned to White House since his departure in January 2021. The two leaders engaged in a nearly two-hour discussion in the Oval Office, focusing on national security, domestic policy, and the transition of government authority. White House press secretary Karine Jean-Pierre described the meeting as "substantive," and President Biden emphasized the importance of a smooth and peaceful transfer of power
